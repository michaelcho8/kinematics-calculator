Kinematic Equations — SUVAT Reference

These four equations relate the five kinematic variables: initial velocity (u), final velocity (v), acceleration (a), displacement (s), and time (t). If you know any three, you can solve for the other two.

v = u + atFinal velocity from initial velocity, acceleration, and time
s = ut + ½at²Displacement when acceleration is constant
v² = u² + 2asRelates velocity and displacement without time
s = ½(u + v)tAverage velocity × time (constant acceleration only)

How projectile motion works

Projectile motion decomposes into two independent components: horizontal (constant velocity, no acceleration) and vertical (accelerated by gravity). The calculator applies these formulas:

  • vₓ = v₀ cos θ — horizontal component
  • vᵧ = v₀ sin θ — vertical component
  • T = 2vᵧ / g — total time of flight
  • H = vᵧ² / 2g — maximum height
  • R = vₓ · T — horizontal range
